Bike

The total length to be biked; 25 miles

This particular race will be a near Half Ironman, consisting of: a run of roughly 16 miles, a challenging swim of 3 miles and a scenic cycling course of 25 miles.

one hour workout This week’s bike workout comes from exercise physiologist, clinical/registered dietitian and USAT Level I coach Marni Sumbal of Trimarni Coaching and Nutrition in Jacksonville, Fla. Like her first trainer workout from November, Sumbal says the goal of this workout is to raise your lactic threshold in order to produce more power — without expending extra energy — so that ultimately you can run strong with good form off the bike.
Warm-up: 15 minutes
6 min easy spin 5×30 sec ON/30 sec OFF high cadence intervals in small chainring.  Cadence should be above 100 RPM without rocking on the saddle during the  ON segments. Upper body should stay still, while the legs do the work.  This will make the time pass quickly during warm-up and to also quickly  elevate the heart rate. It will also set you up for the fun of the main set. OFF should be easy spin. 4 min easy spin, prep mind for main set.
Main set: 33 minutes
8 min ON, 3 min easy spin 6 min ON, 3 min easy spin 4 min ON, 3 min easy spin 2 min ON, 3 min easy spin 1 min ON Same effort as above, ~9 RPE OR slightly above functional threshold.
Cool-down: 12 minutes
Easy spin
